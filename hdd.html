Computer Storage Solutions:

What is a HDD?

A hard disk drive (HDD) is basically a storage device in a computer. It is comprised of multiple metal platters with magnetic coating, spindle, and various moving parts to process and store data. Think of a record player or CD. Platters in a HDD could equate to the record of a record player or a disc to a CD player. This means a HDD is comprised of moving parts, which is why they can fail easily if dropped. It also means that data can be recovered from using simple tools such as "Recuva". The common size for laptop hard drives is the 2.5” model, while a larger 3.5” variant is usually found in desktop computers, see the table below.

What is an SDD?

A solid state drive (SSD) is also another type of data storage that performs the same job as an HDD. But instead of storing data in a magnetic coating on top of platters, an SSD uses flash memory chips and an embedded processor to store, retrieve, and cache data. It is roughly about the same size as a typical HDD, and bears the resemblance of what smartphone batteries would look like. An SSD has no moving parts which means it is faster than a HDD and cannot be damaged from vibrations or a small drop from your hands to the table.

Computer storage facts:

  • Some very low quality solid state drives can almost be slower than a high performance mechanical hard drive.
  • Generally, mechanical hard drives are used for mass storage while SSDs are used for an operating system or other frequently accessed files.
  • Drives can come in many different form factors other than 2.5 and 3.5 inch. Some older drives are larger while some others are small form factor for compact devices.
  • Solid state drives use "NAND" storage, NAND is a chip of storage, usually less than a few centimeters in area while also being anywhere from 2 megabyte to 2 terabyte in capacity.
